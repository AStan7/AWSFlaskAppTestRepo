What is Home Dermatologist?

Home Dermatologist is a technology which increases the capabilities of a home skin cancer assessment. HD uses Artificial Intelligence and image processing to accurately detect if a lesion is cancerous or not. The possible skin lesions it can detect are listed below.

  • Melanoma
  • Basal Cell Carcinoma
  • Benign Keratosis
  • Actinic Keratoses
  • Dermatofibroma
  • Melanocytic Nevi
  • Vascular Lesions

Skin Cancer

Skin Cancer is the most common form of cancer. Here are some skin cancer statistics and details to help in understanding the importance of testing regularly and accurately:

  • 1 in 5 Americans will develop skin cancer by the age of 70
  • More than 2 people die of skin cancer in the U.S. every hour
  • In the U.S., More than 9,500 people are diagnosed with skin cancer every day
  • When detected early, the 5-year survival rate for Melanoma is 99%
  • When Melanoma is detected late, the 5 year survival rate is 27%
  • The American Academy of Dermatology recommends a test once per month
  • There are 4.3 million cases of Basal Cell Carcinoma each year in the USA

Bellow is a graph displaying the incidence rate of Melonoma in Austrailia

These statistics show the importance of regular, accurate skin cancer self assessments. The goal of Home Dermatologist is to try and improve these statistics through innovation and technology.

About Home Dermatologist

Home Dermatologist was created to offer an accurate skin cancer screening technology to try and improve the accuracy of home skin cancer self-assessments and the probability of a user staying motivated to continue testing over time. To achieve this, we use Artificial Intelligence to predict the most likely diagnoses of what the lesion is. The accuracy of the model is 92% and may increase over time as more data is collected and used in the training dataset. Although Home Dermatologist is proven to be accurate, it is important that HD is not seen as a replacement for a professional Dermatologist.
